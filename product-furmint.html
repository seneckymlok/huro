Tasting Notes

Appearance

Brilliant pale gold with greenish reflections. Crystal clear with excellent viscosity indicating good structure and ageing potential.

Nose

Complex aromatic profile with dominant notes of green apple, lemon zest, and white flowers. Secondary aromas include wet stones, almonds, and subtle honey undertones.

Palate

Crisp and vibrant with high acidity perfectly balanced by concentrated fruit flavours. Medium body with mineral-driven finish that lingers beautifully.

Finish

Long, clean finish with lasting citrus notes and a characteristic stony minerality. The acidity ensures a refreshing aftertaste.

Winemaking Process

Vineyard

Our Furmint grapes are sourced from carefully selected vineyards in the Tokaj wine region, situated on volcanic soils at optimal elevations. These ancient vines, some over 30 years old, are planted on south-facing slopes that receive ideal sun exposure.

Harvest

Hand-harvested in late September at perfect ripeness. Only healthy, undamaged grapes are selected by experienced pickers who know the vineyard intimately.

Vinification

Gentle pressing followed by cool fermentation in stainless steel tanks to preserve the wine's natural aromatics and fresh character. Temperature-controlled process lasting 18-21 days.

Ageing

Aged for 6 months on fine lees in stainless steel, with regular batonnage to enhance texture and complexity while maintaining freshness.

The Tokaj Region

The Tokaj wine region, located in northeastern Hungary, is one of the world's most historic wine regions and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. With a winemaking tradition spanning over 1,000 years, Tokaj has earned its reputation as the "Wine of Kings and King of Wines."

The unique terroir of volcanic soils, combined with the region's microclimate created by the convergence of the Bodrog and Tisza rivers, produces wines of exceptional character. The volcanic rhyolite and loess soils impart distinctive mineral qualities to the wines, particularly evident in Furmint.

Climate: Continental with long, warm autumns
Soil: Volcanic rhyolite and loess
UNESCO: World Heritage Site since 2002
History: 1,000+ years of winemaking tradition
